## What to see in San José, Costa Rica

San José is far more than a layover city. Costa Rica's capital has vibrant cultural life, colorful markets, and a food scene that surprises visitors who take the time to explore.

## Must-see attractions

### Central Market (Mercado Central)

The gastronomic heart of the city. Casados, gallo pinto, tamales, and the best coffee in Costa Rica — all in a market with over 130 years of history.

### National Theater (Teatro Nacional)

San José's architectural jewel. Built in 1897, it offers guided tours and regular performances. The neoclassical facade is stunning.

### Pre-Columbian Gold Museum

Over 1,600 gold pieces from indigenous cultures. Located beneath the Plaza de la Cultura, it's one of Central America's finest museums.

### Barrio Escalante

The trendiest neighborhood for restaurants, specialty coffee shops, and art galleries. Gourmet dining at reasonable prices.

### National Museum

Housed in the former Bellavista barracks. Exhibits on natural history, archaeology, and the pre-Columbian stone spheres — a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

## How to get there from Panama

- **Private flight**: 1 h 45 min direct with Sky Ride
- **Commercial flight**: 1 h 15 min + 2 h airport time = 3.5 h total
- **Bus**: Not recommended (18+ hours)

## Tips for your visit

- San José sits at 1,170 meters elevation — the climate is mild, bring a light jacket
- Use Uber or official red taxis for transportation
- Best restaurants are in Barrio Escalante and Barrio Amón
- Most museums are closed on Mondays
