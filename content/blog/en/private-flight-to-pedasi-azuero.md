## Pedásí and the Azuero Peninsula: Panama's Wild Surf Country

230 km southwest of Panama City, the Azuero Peninsula holds some of the country's best-kept secrets: pristine surf beaches, unique Latin American festivals, and one of Panama's premier surfing destinations.

Pedásí, the heart of the peninsula, is a 3,000-person town that has become a cult destination for surfers, divers, and travelers seeking authentic Panama — far from mass tourism.

## Best Beaches Near Pedásí and Azuero

### Playa El Toro
Pedásí's crown jewel. Consistent left-hand breaks throughout surf season (December–March). Long beach, fine sand, and virtually no crowds. The go-to spot for local surfers who don't want to share the wave.

### Playa Los Destiladeros
3 km from Pedásí, this calm beach with natural tide pools at low tide is perfect for snorkeling and swimming. Also a nesting ground for leatherback turtles (Dermochelys coriacea) from October to March.

### Isla Iguana
Nature Reserve 5 km from Pedásí by boat. Intact coral reefs, crystal-clear water, and a colony of frigatebirds and blue-footed boobies. One of the best snorkeling spots on the Panamanian Pacific.

### Playa Venao (El Venao)
The most famous surf spot in Azuero and host of the national surf championship. Consistent quality waves, village surf vibe, and one of the best eco-hotels on the Pacific coast, Eco Venao.

## Top Activities in Pedásí and Azuero

**Turtle Watching** — From October to March, the beaches near Pedásí and Mariato receive leatherback and green turtles to nest. Organized nighttime tours let you observe the process while respecting the animals.

**Diving at Isla Iguana** — The reserve has well-preserved coral reefs, dolphins, and manta rays. Local dive operators offer beginner dives and guided excursions from Pedásí.

**Festival del Toro Guapo** — Azuero's most important festival, held in Guararé in September. Pollera dances, bullfighting, and a full immersion in traditional Panamanian culture. If your dates coincide, don't miss it.

**Azuero Culture** — The Azuero Peninsula is Panama's cultural heartland. The painted hat (sombrero pintado), the pollera dress, and the Festival Nacional de la Mejorana all originate here.

## Travel Tips for Pedásí

- **Best season:** December to April (dry season). June to October has frequent rain but beaches are nearly empty.
- **Cash:** Pedásí has very few ATMs. Bring cash from Panama City.
- **Surfboard rental:** Available in Pedásí town and at Playa Venao from $15/day.
- **Accommodation:** Villa Camilla and Dim's Hostel in town for affordable options. Eco Venao at Playa Venao for glamping.
