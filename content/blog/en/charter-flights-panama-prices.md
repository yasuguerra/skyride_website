## How charter flight pricing works

Charter flights are priced **per aircraft, not per seat**. You pay for the whole plane regardless of whether there are 2 or 5 passengers aboard. This means the more people in your group, the cheaper it gets per person.

The price varies based on:
1. **Destination** — longer routes cost more
2. **Aircraft type** — larger planes with more range cost more per hour
3. **One-way vs. round-trip** — round trips are typically priced at 2× the one-way fare
4. **Timing** — holiday periods (Christmas, Semana Santa) may have premium pricing

## Is a charter flight worth it?

The math works better than most people expect. Let's compare a group trip to San Blas:

**Commercial/overland option:**
- Bus + boat from Panama City to San Blas: $80/person
- Lost travel time: 10–12 hours round trip (two full travel days)
- Physical cost: dehydration, dust, car sickness on mountain roads

**Private charter:**
- Sky Ride charter (C206 to El Porvenir): $706 total / 4 people = **$177/person**
- Travel time: 60 minutes round trip
- Experience: aerial view of the archipelago on arrival

For a group of 4, you're paying less than $100 more per person to save essentially two full travel days and arrive at the islands rested.

## Aircraft options and what they mean for your trip

**Cessna 206** — The workhorse of Panamanian private aviation. 4 passengers + luggage, ideal for island hops under 1 hour. No pressurization needed for these altitudes.

**Piper Azteca** — Twin engine, 5 passengers, slightly more range. The most common aircraft for San Blas and Bocas routes.

**Daher Kodiak / Cessna Caravan** — Larger aircraft, 9–13 passengers. Great for group tours to Contadora or when you need to move more gear (diving equipment, surfboards).

**King Air 200 / King Air F90** — Pressurized turboprops, 6–9 passengers, international range. The go-to for Costa Rica, Medellín, and longer routes. Cabin experience comparable to a regional jet.
