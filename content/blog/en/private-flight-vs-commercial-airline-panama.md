## Private Flight vs. Commercial Airline in Panama: Which Is Right for You?

When people think about flying to San Blas, Bocas del Toro, or David from Panama City, the first instinct is usually to search for a commercial flight. That makes sense — commercial flights appear cheaper at first glance.

But when you do the real math — including total time, comfort, and cost per person — the picture changes. This guide breaks down the differences so you can decide which option fits your trip.

---

## The Honest Comparison: Private vs. Commercial

### 1. Total Travel Time

The biggest misconception about commercial domestic flights in Panama is confusing *flight time* with *total travel time*.

**Commercial flight (example: Panama City → Bocas del Toro):**
- Leave home to the airport: 45–60 min (capital traffic)
- Check-in and security: 60–90 min required in advance
- Possible delays or gate changes: variable
- Flight time: 55 min
- Baggage claim and airport exit: 20 min
- **Real total: 3.5 to 4.5 hours**

**Private flight (Sky Ride, same route):**
- Arrive at Albrook Airfield: 15–20 min before flight
- Immediate boarding with no extended security screening
- Flight time: 1 hour
- Direct landing, no checked baggage wait
- **Real total: 1.5 to 2 hours**

### 2. Actual Cost Per Person

Private flight pricing depends on the aircraft — you pay per plane, not per seat. This completely changes the equation when traveling with more than 3 people.

**Example: 5 people flying to Bocas del Toro (one way)**

| Option | Total Price | Per Person |
|---|---|---|
| Commercial airline (one way) | ~$600–700 | ~$120–140 |
| Private Cessna 206 (5 seats) | $2,200 | **$440 per person** |
| Private Daher Kodiak (9 seats) | $3,200 | **$355 per person** |

The difference is real. But look at what each includes:

**Commercial airline:**
- Fixed schedule (convenient or not)
- Checked baggage may cost extra
- Possible stops or aircraft changes
- No premium seat selection

**Private flight:**
- Fully flexible schedule (depart when you're ready)
- All baggage included
- Guaranteed non-stop flight
- Complete privacy — only your group on board
- Access to smaller airstrips (Pedásí, some Pearl Islands destinations)

### 3. Destinations Reachable

Commercial airlines in Panama operate fixed routes: David, Bocas del Toro, Changuinola, and little else. Private flights can reach **small airstrips** that airlines don't serve:

- Pedásí (Azuero Peninsula)
- Contadora Island (Pearl Islands)
- El Porvenir / Ogobsucum (San Blas)
- Punta Chame
- Rural airstrips in Darién

If you want to reach San Blas on a commercial flight — there is no direct option. By private flight: 30 minutes from Albrook.

### 4. Schedule Flexibility

Commercial flights have fixed departure times (usually early morning for domestic routes). If your meeting runs late, your group gets delayed, or you want to extend your weekend, you can't change the flight without penalties.

On a private flight, **you depart when your group is ready**. With 24–48 hours advance booking, you can adjust departure time to match your schedule.

## When Does Private Flight Make Sense?

Private flight makes the most sense when:
- **4 or more people are traveling** — the per-person cost approaches commercial when split among several passengers
- **The destination has no commercial flight** — San Blas, Pedásí, small islands
- **Time is a critical resource** — business trips, events with exact schedules
- **The experience matters** — weddings, anniversaries, bachelor parties, birthdays
- **Special equipment is involved** — surfboards, dive gear, wheelchairs

Commercial flight makes more sense when:
- You're traveling alone or as a couple
- Budget is tight and timing is flexible
- The destination has a direct connection and convenient schedule
