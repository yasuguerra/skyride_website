## The 5 must-visit islands in San Blas

The Guna Yala archipelago, known as San Blas, is made up of over 365 islands. Most are uninhabited, with coconut palms, white sand, and Caribbean waters that shift from turquoise to deep blue depending on the time of day.

### Dog Island (Isla Perro)

The most popular among international tourists. It features a sunken ship just meters from the beach, perfect for **snorkeling**. Easy access and a relaxed atmosphere.

### Pelican Island (Isla Pelícano)

A small island with an active Guna community. Here you can buy handmade mola textiles directly from artisans and try local food: fresh lobster, coconut, and plantain.

### Needle Island (Isla Aguja)

For those seeking absolute privacy. A tiny island with just a cabin and a coconut tree. The quintessential Robinson Crusoe experience.

### Chichimé Island

A cluster of three islands connected by sandbars. Shallow waters, perfect for walking between islands with water up to your waist.

### Fragata Island

Ideal for groups looking to camp under the stars. Wide beach, great snorkeling visibility, and few visitors outside peak season.

## How to get there by private flight

From Panama City, the flight to San Blas takes **just 30 minutes** in a Cessna 206 or Piper Azteca. Compared to the 4-hour drive (including the dirt road section), the difference is dramatic.

- Departure from Albrook or Marcos A. Gelabert airport
- Landing at Playon Chico or Achutupu airstrip
- Boat transfer to the island of your choice

## How much does it cost?

Private flights to San Blas with Sky Ride start from **$1,250 USD** for up to 5 passengers. Split among the group, it's a competitive option compared to land tours that take all day.

## Practical tips

- Bring cash: there are no ATMs on the islands
- The sun is strong — biodegradable sunscreen is mandatory
- Respect Guna community rules: ask permission before taking photos
- Dry season (December–April) is the best time to visit
