## How to choose your seat on a private airplane

Unlike commercial flights, on a private airplane you have the freedom to choose where to sit. But not all seats are equal — each position has advantages depending on your preferences.

## Typical layout by aircraft

### Cessna 206 (5 seats)

- **Co-pilot**: the best view, next to the pilot
- **Row 2** (2 seats): good balance between view and space
- **Row 3** (2 seats): more legroom, less lateral visibility

### Piper Azteca (5 seats)

- **Co-pilot**: 180° panoramic view
- **Row 2**: the most comfortable seats, wide windows
- **Row 3**: reduced space, ideal for lighter passengers

### Cessna Grand Caravan (12 seats)

- **Co-pilot**: the premium experience
- **Front rows**: less engine noise, better visibility
- **Back rows**: more space, but more vibration

### King Air (6–9 seats)

- **Club seating** (facing seats): perfect for in-flight meetings
- **Individual seats**: window side with fold-down table
- **Pressurized**: smoother flight at higher altitude

## Tips based on your priority

| If you want... | Choose... |
|----------------|-----------|
| Best view | Co-pilot seat |
| Less turbulence | Center seats (over the wings) |
| More space | Back row in Cessna/Piper |
| Photos/video | Right-side window (coastal views en route to Contadora) |
| Conversation | Club seating in King Air |

## Can you request the co-pilot seat?

Yes. At Sky Ride, if weight and balance permit, you can request to sit next to the pilot. It's the most exciting experience on a private flight.
